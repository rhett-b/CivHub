Quick Note: When Links Break, Trust Does Too

Published April 10, 2025

Over 25% of U.S. government URLs break within five years. It's not just an inconvenience — it's a slow erosion of public memory and access. When critical information disappears from the public web, it becomes harder to hold institutions accountable, or even understand how decisions were made. Digital decay is a policy problem in disguise.
